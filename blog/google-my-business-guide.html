Google My Business: The Most Underrated Tool in Kenya

SB

SmartBiz SEO Team

When you are hungry in Nairobi and you don't know where to eat, what do you do? You open Google Maps and search "Restaurants near me."

When your car breaks down in Thika, what do you do? You search "Mechanic near me."

In 2026, Google isn't just a search engine; it is the Yellow Pages, the Map, and the Review Board all rolled into one. The tool that powers these results is the Google Business Profile (GBP), formerly known as Google My Business.

It is completely free. Yet, millions of Kenyan businesses have unclaimed profiles, wrong phone numbers, or no photos. If you are not optimizing this profile, you are handing customers directly to your competitors.

1. Why "Near Me" Searches Are Gold

Consumer behavior in Kenya has shifted. We want convenience. We want things that are close.

When someone searches with "near me" or a specific location (e.g., "Salon in Kilimani"), they have High Purchase Intent. They are not browsing for fun; they are looking to go there and spend money immediately.

Ranking in the "Local Pack" (the top 3 map results) gets you 70% of the clicks. Being #1 on Maps is often more valuable than being #1 on standard search.

2. The Verification Headache (And How to Solve It)

In the past, Google sent a postcard from the USA to verify your address. In Kenya, with our complex postal system, these cards never arrived.

In 2026, Google has switched to Video Verification. This is where most business owners get stuck.

How to pass Video Verification on the first try:
You need to record one continuous video (no editing) that shows:
1. Your street sign or building name.
2. You walking into the building.
3. You unlocking the door (proof of access).
4. Your marketing materials (business cards/branding).
5. Your tools of trade (computers, stock, equipment).

If you work from home or are a mobile service provider (like a plumber), you must hide your address and verify as a "Service Area Business."

3. Optimization: Filling the "Empty Spaces"

Google rewards completeness. A profile that is 100% filled out ranks higher than one that is 50% filled out.

  • Categories: Don't just pick one. Pick a primary category (e.g., "Restaurant") and secondary categories (e.g., "Pizza Delivery," "Cafe," "Bar").
  • Products: Did you know you can upload your catalogue to Google? When people search for "Cement," your hardware store listing can show the price and photo of the cement directly on Google.
  • Q&A: Don't wait for people to ask questions. Ask your own questions (e.g., "Do you have parking?") and answer them yourself. This populates the FAQ section.

4. Reviews: The Currency of Trust

In Kenya, we don't trust advertisements. We trust other people. A business with 50 reviews (even if the rating is 4.5) looks more trustworthy than a business with 2 reviews of 5.0.

The Strategy: You need a system to get reviews.

  • QR Codes: Print a QR code that links directly to your review page and stick it on your counter or reception desk.
  • Incentives for Staff: Tell your waiters or sales staff: "For every 5-star review that mentions your name, you get a KES 200 bonus." Watch how fast your reviews grow.

Responding to Reviews: Reply to every single review. If it's positive, say thanks. If it's negative, apologize and offer to fix it offline. This shows Google you are active.

5. Treat it Like Social Media

Most people set up their profile and forget it. But Google allows you to post Updates.

You can post "Offer Updates," "Event Updates," or "What's New." These posts appear right under your listing. They expire after 6 months, so keep them fresh. Posting weekly signals to the algorithm that you are alive and open for business.

6. Insights: Knowing Your Customer

The backend of Google Business Profile gives you incredible data:

  • How many people called you directly from the listing?
  • How many asked for directions?
  • What keywords did they type to find you?

If you see people are finding you by searching "Cheap Laptops" but you only sell "Premium MacBooks," you know you need to adjust your description to filter your audience.

Conclusion

Google Business Profile is the digital front door to your shop. It is free, effective, and essential. In 2026, if a customer cannot find you on Maps, to them, you do not exist.
