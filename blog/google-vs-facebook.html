Google Ads vs. Facebook Ads: Where to Spend Your First 10k in Kenya

SB

SmartBiz Marketing Desk

You have started a business in Nairobi. You have a logo, a small stock of products, and exactly KES 10,000 left for marketing. You know you need to advertise online, but you are stuck at a crossroads.

Do you give that money to Google or Facebook (Meta)?

This is the most common question we get at SmartBiz. The answer isn't "it depends." The answer lies in understanding User Intent. In 2026, the digital landscape in Kenya has matured, and choosing the wrong platform will burn your budget in less than 48 hours.

Let's break down the mechanics of both, specifically for the Kenyan market.

1. The Fundamental Difference: Search vs. Scroll

Before spending a shilling, understand the mindset of the user.

Google Ads = "I Need It Now" (Active Intent)

When someone goes to Google and types "Emergency plumber Nairobi" or "Car battery delivery Kilimani", they have a problem, and they have money in their hand. They are looking for a solution right now.

Google Ads puts you in front of people who are already shopping. You don't need to convince them they need a plumber; you just need to convince them to pick you.

Facebook/Instagram Ads = "That Looks Nice" (Passive Discovery)

When someone is scrolling through Instagram or Facebook, they are not looking for a plumber. They are looking for memes, news, or their friends' wedding photos.

Your ad interrupts them. You have to convince them to stop scrolling, care about your product, and click. This is best for things people didn't know they wantedâ€”like a cool pair of sneakers, a weekend getaway deal, or a new gadget.

2. The Cost Comparison in Kenya (2026 Data)

Budget is everything for an SME. Here is what KES 10,000 gets you on each platform.

Metric Facebook / Instagram Google Search Ads
Cost Model Usually pay per Impression (CPM) Pay per Click (CPC)
Avg. Cost (Kenya) KES 5 - 20 per click KES 40 - 150+ per click
Reach Huge (Thousands of people) Lower (Only people searching)
Conversion Rate Lower (1% - 3%) Higher (5% - 15%)
The Takeaway: Facebook gets you more eyeballs for your money. Google gets you fewer eyeballs, but they are much more likely to buy immediately.

3. Where to Spend Your First KES 10,000

We have developed a simple rule based on what you sell. Do not guess. Follow this framework:

Scenario A: You Sell a Service (Emergency or Need-Based)

Examples: Plumbers, Mechanics, Dentists, Lawyers, Borehole Drilling.

Verdict: Google Ads.

Nobody goes to Instagram to search for a lawyer. They go to Google. If you spend your 10k on Instagram, you will get likes but no calls. Put it all on Google Search Ads targeting specific keywords like "Dentist in Westlands."

Scenario B: You Sell a Product (Visual or Lifestyle)

Examples: Fashion, Shoes, Furniture, Real Estate, Food, Electronics.

Verdict: Facebook & Instagram Ads.

People need to see the shoes to want them. Google Search text ads are boring for fashion. Use Instagram Reels ads to show off the product. Target by interest (e.g., "Interested in Sneakers").

Scenario C: You Sell B2B Solutions

Examples: Office Supplies, Software, Corporate Training.

Verdict: LinkedIn (Expensive) or Google Ads.

Facebook can work if you target business owners, but it's harder. Google Ads targeting "Office furniture suppliers Nairobi" is your safest bet.

4. The "M-Pesa Integration" Factor

In 2026, both platforms have improved, but user behavior is key.

  • Facebook/IG: Works great with a "Click to WhatsApp" button. The user sees the shoe, clicks the button, lands in your WhatsApp, and you close the deal via M-Pesa. This is the most popular funnel in Kenya.
  • Google: Works best with a "Click to Call" button or sending traffic to a website. If you don't have a good website, Google Ads will fail.

5. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Boosting Posts: Don't use the blue "Boost Post" button on Facebook. Use the Ads Manager backend. The Boost button is optimized for "Likes," not "Sales."
  • Broad Targeting on Google: Don't bid on the keyword "Shoes." You will go broke in an hour competing with Jumia. Bid on "Men's leather formal shoes Nairobi." Long-tail keywords save money.
  • Ignoring Retargeting: The money is in the follow-up. Install the Meta Pixel on your site so you can show ads to people who visited but didn't buy.

Conclusion

If you have KES 10,000 and you sell services, give it to Google. If you sell products, give it to Mark Zuckerberg. But whatever you do, track your results. If you put in KES 1 and don't get KES 3 back, pause the ad and fix your offer.
