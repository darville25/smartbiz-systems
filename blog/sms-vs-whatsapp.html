SMS Marketing vs. WhatsApp: Which ROI is Higher?

SB

SmartBiz Marketing Desk

Every Kenyan business owner faces this dilemma: You have a database of 1,000 customer phone numbers. You want to tell them about your Easter Sale. Do you send a Bulk SMS or a WhatsApp Broadcast?

The answer is not as simple as "WhatsApp is free" (it isn't for business) or "SMS is cheap." Both channels serve very different purposes in the Kenyan market.

1. The Case for Bulk SMS

SMS is the "Hammer." It is blunt, effective, and works on every phone, from the latest iPhone 15 to the humble "Kabambe" (feature phone).

Pros of SMS

  • 98% Open Rate: Almost every SMS gets read within 3 minutes.
  • No Internet Needed: Reaches customers in remote areas or those without bundles.
  • Cost Effective: Roughly KES 0.3 to 0.8 per SMS depending on volume.

2. The Case for WhatsApp Business API

WhatsApp is the "Scalpel." It is precise, engaging, and rich.

Pros of WhatsApp

  • Rich Media: Send photos of your products, PDFs, and location pins.
  • Trust: A verified business profile builds confidence.
  • Two-Way Convo: Customers can reply, ask questions, and buy instantly. SMS is usually one-way.

3. The Cost Battle (KES)

Let's do the math for a campaign to 1,000 people.

Scenario A: Bulk SMS

1,000 SMS x KES 0.5 = KES 500.

This is cheap. But you cannot track who opened it. You only know if they clicked a link (if they have bundles).

Scenario B: WhatsApp Marketing Message

Meta charges per "Marketing Conversation." In Kenya, this is roughly KES 0.80 - 1.50 per initiated conversation.

1,000 Messages x KES 1.2 = KES 1,200.

It is more expensive. BUT, the engagement rate is typically 5-10x higher than SMS.

4. The "Rule of Thumb" for 2025

When should you use which?

  • Use SMS for Alerts: Delivery notifications ("Your rider is here"), OTPs, Payment confirmations, Urgent 24hr offers. Winner: SMS
  • Use WhatsApp for Sales: New collection launch (send photos), educational content, nurturing leads, high-ticket items. Winner: WhatsApp

Conclusion

Smart businesses in Kenya use a Hybrid Strategy. Send a low-cost SMS to your cold list to wake them up. Move the interested ones to WhatsApp for the conversion.

Don't rely on just one channel. The market is too competitive for that.
